About Lions

The lion is a species in the family Felidae and a member of the genus Panthera. They have a muscular, deep-chested body, short, rounded head, round ears, and a hairy tuft at the end of its tail. They are commonly found in Africa, below the Sahara desert.

Types of Lions

There is only one species of lion, which is known scientifically as Panthera leo. There are two recognised subspecies, the African lion P. l. leo and the Asiatic lion P. l. persica.

Female Lion

Mother Lion carrying her cub

Female lions are the primary hunters of the group. They are smaller and more agile than males. But since their prey is still generally faster than them, they use teamwork to bring an animal down. Fanning out, they form a semicircle, with the smaller, weaker lionesses herding the prey towards the center.

As animalfactguide.com states: Lions usually hunt at night. Their prey includes antelopes, buffaloes, zebras, young elephants, rhinos, hippos, wild hogs, crocodiles and giraffes. But they also sometimes eat smaller prey like mice, birds, hares, lizards, and tortoises. They are not above stealing kills from other carnivores, like hyenas, wild dogs, cheetahs, and leopards, or scavenging spoiled meat. (source).
